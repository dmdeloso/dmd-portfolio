July 2021, San Francisco Chronicle

The Jessica Simulation: Love and loss in the age of A.I.

I designed and developed a longform feature chronicling one man's attempt to process his fiancee's death using an A.I. chatbot. Using Gatsby and React, I created elements including animated terminal windows, changing background colors and an animated life bar to put the reader in the subject's shoes. This story went viral on Twitter after it published online and even had film and TV rights sold to Universal.

June 2021, San Francisco Chronicle

Lift Every Voice

I developed the hub pages for Lift Every Voice, a Juneteenth project by Hearst Newspapers which tells the stories of Black elders across the country. I collaborated with a central design team to produce a project template which each Hearst market could modify and deploy individually.

May 2021, San Francisco Chronicle

24 on 24: Willie Mays by the numbers

To commemorate MLB Legend Willie Mays' 90th birthday, I designed and developed a project listing 24 facts about his life and career. I used React to produce an animated slideshow which can be navigated using arrow keys, a mouse wheel or on-screen buttons.

January 2021, North by Northwestern

When2Eat in Evanston

I used Leaflet and D3 to produce an interactive map that tells readers which Evanston restaurants are open at any given time. The project pulls data from a CSV file which can be updated to easily add or modify restaurant information without directly editing the HTML or JavaScript code.
